Description

Spaghetti alla carbonara. Luscious and wonderfully indulgent, pasta carbonara takes as long to make as it does to cook the pasta. The ingredients are simpleâ€”just spaghetti (or another long pasta), and the carbonara is made with pancetta or bacon, eggs, Parmesan, a little olive oil, salt and pepper. The silky carbonara sauce is created when the beaten eggs are tossed with the hot pasta and a little fat from the pancetta or bacon. Did I already mention indulgent? Yes, this is not a make-it-everyday recipe. This is an I've-been-eating-my-kale-for-weeks-and-now-I-want-to-splurge recipe. But heck, if you are going to splurge, you may as well do it right with pasta carbonara.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil or unsalted butter
  • 1/2 pound pancetta or thick cut bacon, diced
  • 1 to 2 garlic cloves, minced, about 1 teaspoon (optional)
  • 3 to 4 whole eggs
  • 1 cup grated parmesan or pecorino cheese
  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Put a large pot of salted water to boil. (1 tablespoon of salt for every 2 quarts of water)
  2. Saute the pancetta (or bacon) and garlic. Transfer to a bowl.
  3. In a small bowl, beat the eggs and half of the cheese
  4. Once the water has reached a rolling boil, cook the pasta.
  5. Toss the pasta with the pancetta or bacon in the bowl.
  6. Add the beaten egg mixture.
  7. Serve immediately with parmesan and black pepper.
